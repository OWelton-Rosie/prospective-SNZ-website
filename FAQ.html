Frequently Asked Questions

Speedcubing is the practice of competitively solving puzzles such as the Rubik's Cube as fast as possible.

Speedcubing competitions are sanctioned by the World Cube Association (WCA) and organised by members of the local community.

You can participate by registering for competitions on Speedcubing New Zealand's website. Visit our Competitions page to view all of New Zealand's upcoming competitions.

Speedcubing New Zealand (SNZ) is New Zealand's WCA-accredited Regional Organization. We fund and help organise competitions held in New Zealand.

The World Cube Association (WCA) is the international governing body of speedcubing. The WCA manages the WCA Regulations and uploads the results from competitions, amoung other duties.

The World Cube Association (WCA) sets the rules for official competitions. You can read the WCA Regulations here.

SNZ does not organise competitions. Competitions are organised by members of the local speedcubing community. To find out more about organising a competition, please reach out to us.
